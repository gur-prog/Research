# Methodical Innovation

## Goal

One or two sentences on what you want to learn and why. Be specific about the outcome — "understand X well enough to do Y".

## Follow-up goal

What will you actually *do* with this research once it's done? Be concrete — "start a 12-week cutting phase", "rewrite my kid's bedtime routine", "decide whether to switch jobs". This is the single most important input for the product-builder: products are shaped to serve this goal, not to be neutral encyclopedias.

Example: *"Use the rulebook and meal plan to run a 90-day pilot — I'll follow it strictly and track weight, energy, and sleep quality. If the pilot works, I'll make it permanent."*

## Research questions

The specific questions this research must answer well. Source-scout will treat these as a coverage checklist — each question should have at least one strong-trust source before the gather phase can claim to be done. If the questions can't all be answered after a gather round, the open-questions file will flag which ones are still unanswered.

Good questions are **specific and decidable**: "How much protein per kg bodyweight for a 40-year-old doing 3x/week resistance training?" beats "Is protein important?".
