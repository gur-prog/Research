# Minimal Dose Resistance Exercise

## Why this source

*Sports Medicine* 2024 overview defining the literal floor for resistance training that still produces strength gains. Completes the Q2 answer with a concrete time budget for busy students.

## Content

**Publication:** Nuzzo et al., *Sports Medicine*, March 2024

### Absolute Minimum for Strength Gains (Untrained Adults)

- **Frequency:** 1 day/week
- **Sets:** 1 set per exercise
- **Intensity:** 60–85% of maximum load
- **Exercises:** 4+ per session

> One session of resistance exercise per week, consisting of four or more exercises of eccentric–concentric repetitions per session, improves muscle strength.

### Volume vs. Frequency

"Differences in gains in muscle size and strength from different exercise frequencies disappear when exercise volume is equated between conditions." **Frequency matters less than total weekly volume.**

### Observed Outcomes

- Weekend warrior (1 session/week): 6–74% strength gains across studies
- Single-set programs (2–3x/week): ~20% strength gains, comparable to multi-set programs

### Time-Efficiency Recommendations

Three approaches for busy populations:
1. **Weekend Warrior** — all training in one weekly session
2. **Single-set resistance** — 1 set × 7–10 exercises × 2–3 sessions/week (~30 min each)
3. **Exercise "snacks"** — 2–10 minute daily bouts at home

> Something is better than nothing for those unable to meet standard guidelines.
