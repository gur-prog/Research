# Multi-Behavior Change Interventions — Meta-Analysis

## Why this source

*Annals of Behavioral Medicine* 2024 systematic review + meta-analysis of multi-behavior interventions — directly answers Q6 on whether changing several habits at once is tractable, and which combinations yield the best results.

## Content

**Citation:** Silva et al., *Annals of Behavioral Medicine*, June 2024

### Core Finding

Targeting more than one behavior at a time **is effective** in chronic disease management. The review studied interventions changing 2+ behaviors simultaneously (it did not head-to-head compare with single-behavior interventions).

### Most Studied Combinations

- **Diet + physical activity + smoking cessation** (k = 20 studies)
- **Diet + physical activity** (k = 16 studies)

These clusters dominated cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and hypertension intervention literature.

### Effect Sizes at Intervention End

- Physical activity: d = 0.177 (small)
- Diet (fat intake): d = 0.436 (moderate)
- Fruit/vegetable intake: d = 0.232 (small)
- Medication adherence: d = 0.373 (small-to-moderate)
- **Smoking: d = −0.019 (no effect)**

For dichotomous outcomes, risk ratios ranged 1.026–2.001 across most behaviors (except smoking).

### Notable Gap

The review did NOT identify the specific behavior change techniques or mechanisms driving effectiveness — "beyond the review's scope". So the *what works best* question (goal-setting vs. self-monitoring vs. feedback) remains open.
