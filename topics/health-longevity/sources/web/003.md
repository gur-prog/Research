# Combined Lifestyle Factors and Mortality — Meta-Analysis

## Why this source

A 142-study meta-analysis (2.58M participants) quantifying the combined effect of healthy lifestyle factors on all-cause and cardiovascular mortality — establishes that the bundle matters more than any single factor.

## Content

**Authors & Date:** Yan-Bo Zhang et al., published January 2021 in *Journal of Epidemiology and Community Health*

### Lifestyle Factors Examined
The analysis investigated combinations of at least three factors: cigarette smoking, alcohol consumption, physical activity, diet, and overweight/obesity status.

### Key Effect Sizes (Healthiest vs. Least-Healthy Lifestyles)

- **All-cause mortality:** HR=0.45 (95% CI 0.41–0.48; 74 studies, 2.58M participants)
- **Cardiovascular mortality:** HR=0.42 (95% CI 0.37–0.46; 41 studies, 1.74M participants)
- **Incident cardiovascular disease:** HR=0.38 (95% CI 0.29–0.51; 22 studies, 755K participants)
- **CVD subtypes:** HRs ranged from 0.29 to 0.45

### Population Impact

The study included 142 prospective cohort studies and found these protective associations were "largely significant and consistent among individuals from different continents, racial groups and socioeconomic backgrounds."

### Dose-Response Findings

The abstract does not specify explicit dose-response relationships, only comparative risk reduction between healthiest and unhealthiest lifestyle groups.

## Notable quotes
