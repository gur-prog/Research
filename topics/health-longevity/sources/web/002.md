# WHO 2020 Guidelines on Physical Activity and Sedentary Behaviour

## Why this source

The canonical global guideline for how much and what kind of movement adults need, built on GRADE-methodology systematic reviews — the single most authoritative source for exercise dosing.

## Content

**Publication:** *British Journal of Sports Medicine*, November 23, 2020
**Lead Author:** Fiona C Bull (World Health Organization)

### Key Recommendations for Adults (18–64 years)

**Aerobic Physical Activity:**
Adults should engage in "150–300 min of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity, or at least 75–150 min of vigorous-intensity" per week, or an equivalent combination. This represents a significant update from the 2010 guidelines, which specified minimum thresholds only.

**Resistance Training:**
"Muscle-strengthening activities at moderate or greater intensity that involve all major muscle groups on 2 or more days a week" are recommended for additional health benefits.

**Sedentary Behavior:**
Adults should "limit the amount of time spent being sedentary." The guidelines note that replacing sedentary time with any physical activity intensity — even light activity — provides health benefits.

### Dose-Response Findings

The guidelines document a curvilinear relationship where health benefits occur across activity levels, with "relative benefits tend to diminish at higher levels." However, the document states there is "insufficient evidence to specify quantitative thresholds of sedentary behaviour."

### Evidence Base

The recommendations are based on systematic reviews using GRADE methodology, with overall moderate-certainty evidence supporting adult aerobic and resistance activity guidelines as strong recommendations.

## Notable quotes
