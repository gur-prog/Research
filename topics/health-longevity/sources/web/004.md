# Nutrition and Cognitive Health: A Life Course Approach

## Why this source

Peer-reviewed *Frontiers in Public Health* 2023 review linking specific nutrients, food groups, and dietary patterns to cognitive performance — directly answers Q1 on whether a longevity-aligned diet also supports focus for long study sessions.

## Content

**Publication:** Puri, Shaheen, & Grover (2023), *Frontiers in Public Health*, March 27

### Nutrients Linked to Cognitive Function

**Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):** Modifies neurotransmission, reduces neuro-inflammation, promotes neuronal survival and neurogenesis. DHA is crucial for neurogenesis, neuronal migration, and synaptogenesis affecting attention and impulse control.

**B-Vitamins:** Essential for brain development and myelination. B-12 particularly important; low folate linked to mild cognitive impairment risk.

**Vitamin D:** Low serum levels associated with impaired cognition. High-dose D3 (4,000 IU/day) improved visuospatial memory in healthy adults.

**Iron:** Critical for oxygen transport; deficiency impairs cognitive development. Iron overload also damages neurological function.

**Polyphenols (flavonoids):** Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory action. Cocoa flavonoids associated with 41% lower cognitive decline risk.

### Dietary Patterns

**Most protective:**
- Mediterranean diet — lower dementia prevalence, lower cognitive decline risk
- MIND diet (Mediterranean-DASH for neurodegenerative delay)
- Nordic and DASH diets — similar protective effects
- Asian plant-based patterns — slower cognitive decline

**Specific foods:** Fish protective against cognitive decline and dementia. Whole grains (vs. refined) associated with better cognition. Nuts, berries, olive oil, avocados delay cognitive decline. **High-fat dairy associated with cognitive decline.**

### Macronutrient Patterns

- Low-fat diets protective against cognitive decline
- High saturated fat → worse cognitive trajectories
- High-protein evidence inconsistent
- High energy from protein/fat + low carbohydrate → later-life cognitive decline

### Gut-Brain Axis

Dietary fibers and probiotics benefit cognition via microbiome. Gut bacteria produce neurotransmitters (GABA, serotonin, dopamine).

### Practical Recommendations

- Minimum 400g daily fruits and vegetables
- <10% total energy from free sugars
- <30% energy from fat; prioritize unsaturated sources
- Mediterranean-style whole-diet approach > isolated nutrients
- Regular physical activity combined with healthy diet

## Notable quotes

> Young adulthood and middle age (40s-50s) are crucial windows for preventive nutrition interventions before dementia development.
