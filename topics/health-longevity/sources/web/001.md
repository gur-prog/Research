# Healthy dietary patterns, longevity genes, and life expectancy: A prospective cohort study

## Why this source

Peer-reviewed prospective cohort in *Science Advances* (2026) quantifying life-expectancy gains from five major healthy dietary patterns, independent of genetics — strongest possible evidence that diet choice alone moves the needle.

## Content

**Authors & Publication:** Yanling Lv et al., *Science Advances*, February 13, 2026

### Dietary Patterns Evaluated
Five scoring systems were assessed:
- Alternate Healthy Eating Index-2010 (AHEI)
- Alternate Mediterranean Diet (AMED)
- Healthful Plant-based Diet Index (hPDI)
- Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension (DASH)
- Diabetes Risk Reduction Diet (DRRD)

### Foods Emphasized
All patterns prioritized "vegetables, fruits, and whole grains, which are rich in dietary fiber" and nuts with unsaturated fatty acids.

### Foods Limited
The patterns discouraged "sugar-sweetened beverages" and high glycemic index foods.

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Mortality reduction:** 18–24% lower all-cause mortality risk comparing highest to lowest dietary adherence
- **Life expectancy gains at age 45:**
  - Men: 1.9 to 3.0 years
  - Women: 1.5 to 2.3 years
- **Strongest associations:** DRRD showed strongest results for males; AMED for females

### Key Recommendations
Healthy dietary patterns extend life expectancy "regardless of longevity genes," offering individuals flexibility to adapt patterns "according to their preferences and traditions."
