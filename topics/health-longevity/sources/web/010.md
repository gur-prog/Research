# Energy Drinks and Student Sleep

## Why this source

Large daily-diary study (667 college students, 25,616 person-days) quantifying energy drink effects on sleep — directly answers Q7 on common student pitfalls, specifically the energy-drink → bad-sleep trap.

## Content

**Authors & Date:** Patrick et al., *Behavioral Sleep Medicine*, May 16, 2016

### Primary Effects of Energy Drink Consumption

**Sleep duration:** On days when students consumed energy drinks, they reported lower sleep quantity that night — approximately **0.40 hours (~24 minutes)** less sleep vs. non-consumption days.

**Sleep quality:** Significantly reduced sleep quality on energy-drink days, robust after controlling for average individual usage.

**Next-day tiredness:** Greater next-day tiredness following energy drink consumption, effects robust across analytical models.

### Critical Finding — Low-Frequency Use Still Matters

About 46.6% of participants reported energy drink use on at least one sampled day. Effects persisted "controlling for average levels" of personal consumption — meaning **even occasional use hurts sleep**, not just habitual users.

### Proposed Mechanism — "Stimulation-Sedation Loop"

Caffeine simultaneously contributes to sleep debt (via delayed sleep onset and fragmented architecture) while users consume more energy drinks to combat the pre-existing tiredness this causes — creating a self-reinforcing cycle.

### Study Scale

- 667 college students
- 25,616 person-days of real-world (non-lab) data
- Within-person and between-person models both significant

## Notable quotes

> Even low-frequency use showed measurable effects.
