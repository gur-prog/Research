# Occupational Sitting, Leisure Activity, and Mortality

## Why this source

*JAMA Network Open* 2024 cohort of 481,688 adults (mean follow-up 12.85 years) directly addressing whether leisure activity can offset occupational sitting — the central question for anyone spending long hours at an EE study desk.

## Content

**Citation:** Gao W, Sanna M, Chen Y, et al. *JAMA Network Open*, January 19, 2024

### Primary Hazard Ratios

Individuals predominantly sitting at work vs. mostly non-sitting:
- **All-cause mortality:** HR 1.16 (95% CI 1.11–1.20) — 16% higher
- **Cardiovascular mortality:** HR 1.34 (95% CI 1.22–1.46) — 34% higher

### Physical Activity Offset

Leisure-time physical activity substantially mitigates occupational sitting risk. **15–30 minutes/day of additional physical activity offsets sitting-related mortality risks.**

- Those with low activity (<15 min/day) needed **+30 min/day** additional
- Those with 15–29 min/day needed **+15 min/day** additional
- Personal Activity Intelligence (PAI) score >100 notably reduced elevated risks

### Study Population

481,688 participants (mean age 39.3, 53.2% female), Taiwan health surveillance 1996–2017.

### Recommendations for Desk Workers

Authors advocate:
- Alternating between sitting and non-sitting at work
- Standing tables / activity-permissive workstations
- Employer-sponsored physical activity programs

## Notable quotes

> Individuals who mostly sit at work engaging in low LTPA were less at risk than the reference group.
