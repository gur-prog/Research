# Mindfulness and Cognitive Functioning — Meta-Analysis of 111 RCTs

## Why this source

Meta-analysis of 111 RCTs (n = tens of thousands) testing whether mindfulness moves real cognitive outcomes — directly answers Q5 on stress/recovery practices for both longevity and sustained focus.

## Content

**Authors & Publication:** Nur Hani Zainal & Michelle G. Newman, *Health Psychology Review*, August 2023

### Primary Effect Sizes

**Global Cognition:**
- Small-to-large effects vs. waitlist/no-treatment: g = 0.583
- Small effects vs. active controls: g = 0.208

**Executive function components** (vs. waitlist / vs. active):
- Executive attention: 0.301 / 0.192
- Working memory accuracy: 0.326 / 0.296
- Inhibition accuracy: 0.643 / 0.192
- Shifting accuracy: 0.272 / 0.200

**Sustained Attention:** g = 0.367 (accuracy)

**Subjective Cognitive Functioning:** g = 0.257 (vs. waitlist); 0.394 (vs. active)

### No Significant Effects

Mindfulness had no meaningful impact on latency-based indices, processing speed, verbal fluency, episodic memory, or cognitive errors.

### Moderating Factors

- **Clinical status:** Larger effects in populations with elevated psychiatric symptoms vs. healthy controls
- **Delivery mode:** Face-to-face produced stronger effects on global cognition and sustained attention than self-guided apps
- **Dose-response:** No significant moderation by session number or treatment duration — **brief interventions showed comparable benefits**
- **MBI type:** Non-standard, population-tailored approaches outperformed standard MBSR/MBCT for sustained attention

### Mechanism

Authors posit that mindfulness enhances accuracy-based executive function by improving self-regulation and reducing mind-wandering.

## Notable quotes

> Brief interventions showed comparable benefits [to longer programs].
