# Dose-Response: Aerobic + Muscle-Strengthening PA and Mortality

## Why this source

Large (n=416,420) national cohort pinpointing the actual minimum effective dose for aerobic and resistance training against mortality. Directly answers Q2 on time-efficient exercise for people with heavy study/work loads.

## Content

**Publication:** Coleman et al., *British Journal of Sports Medicine*, August 2022 (PMC article)

### Minimum Effective Dose — Aerobic Activity

**1 hour/week of aerobic PA** produced substantial mortality risk reduction:
- HR 0.85 (95% CI 0.83–0.86)
- Benefits plateaued at ~3 hours/week
- Even doses below WHO guidelines (150 min/week) showed clinically meaningful reductions

### Dose-Response Shape

Steep inverse curve up to ~3 hours/week aerobic, then flattens. Additional volume beyond 3 hours gave minimal extra benefit.

### Muscle-Strengthening Exercise

- Marked inverse association with mortality risk at just **1 session/week**
- Minimal additional benefit beyond **1–2 sessions/week**
- At 7 sessions/week, benefits disappeared (HR 0.99, 95% CI 0.94–1.04)

### Combined Effects

Pairing muscle-strengthening with aerobic conferred additional mortality risk reduction, but the minimum effective dose for aerobic activity remained **1 hour/week**.

### Age/Sex Variations

Little difference between males and females. Older adults (60+) showed slightly stronger associations extending beyond 3 hours/week.

## Notable quotes

> Even doses below WHO recommendations (150 min/week moderate intensity) demonstrated clinically meaningful mortality reductions.
