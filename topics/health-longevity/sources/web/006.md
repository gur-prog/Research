# Sleep Disorders and Cognitive Decline — Meta-Analysis

## Why this source

2025 systematic review + meta-analysis of longitudinal studies quantifying sleep–cognition risk across sleep disorders and durations. Directly answers Q3 on sleep dosing for both longevity and cognitive performance.

## Content

**Authors & Date:** Zhang et al., 2025

### Effect Sizes (Relative Risks)

- **Insomnia:** RR 1.13 for dementia risk
- **Short sleep (<7h):** RR 1.27 for cognitive decline
- **Long sleep (>8h):** RR 1.43 for all-cause dementia; RR 1.66 for Alzheimer's disease
- **Excessive daytime sleepiness:** RR 1.85 for vascular dementia; 1.41 for all-cause dementia
- **Sleep-related movement disorders:** RR 2.53 for vascular dementia (strongest association)
- **Poor sleep quality:** RR 1.24 for Alzheimer's disease

### U-Shaped Relationship

Non-linear: both insufficient and excessive sleep increase dementia risk. Optimal window: **7–8 hours**. Long sleep (>8h) shows stronger associations with cognitive decline than short sleep.

### Recommendations

Authors frame "sleep management" as a modifiable prevention factor and advocate systematic screening and early intervention for sleep disturbances. Specific duration targets beyond the 7–8 hour window were not prescribed.

## Notable quotes

> Sleep management should be treated as a modifiable prevention factor.
