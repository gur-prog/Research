# Cognitive-Behavioral Sleep Interventions for Healthy Adults

## Why this source

Sleep Medicine Reviews meta-analysis of 11 RCTs specifically in adults **without** diagnosed sleep disorders — the right population for the user's question on what improves sleep for a healthy student. Directly answers Q4.

## Content

### Overall effect

**Hedge's g = −0.54 (95% CI −0.90 to −0.19, p < 0.01)** — medium effect on sleep quality (PSQI), meaningful in a healthy population.

### Intervention components used in effective trials

The four components appearing most frequently across effective trials:

1. **Stress management and relaxation practice**
2. **Stimulus control** (bed only for sleep; leave if not sleeping)
3. **Sleep hygiene education** (light, caffeine, consistent wake time, environment)
4. **Exercise**

### Key moderator

**Baseline sleep health was the only significant effect moderator** (p = 0.01) — interventions worked better for people with worse baseline sleep. People who already sleep well have less room to improve.

### Study scale

18,009 records screened → 11 RCTs included. 7 of 11 used PSQI as the primary outcome.

## Notable quotes

> Interventions targeting cognitive and behavioral self-regulation improve sleep quality in adults without clinical sleep disorder.

> Baseline sleep health was the only significant effect moderator.
