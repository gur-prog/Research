# Natural Short Sleeper Genes — Individual Variation in Sleep Need

## Why this source

Systematic review of the genetic mutations (DEC2, ADRB1, NPSR1, GRM1/mGluR1) associated with natural short sleeping — directly answers Q1 on whether the optimal sleep window is universal or subjective. Establishes that a subset of humans genuinely need less sleep and thrive on it.

## Content

**Natural short sleepers (NSS)** average 4–6 hours/night "without any negative impact on health or daytime drowsiness," vs. standard ~8h recommendations.

### Carrier vs non-carrier differences

- **DEC2 (P385R):** Family members with mutation averaged **6.25 hours** vs. **8.06 hours** in non-carriers.
- **ADRB1 (A187V):** Carriers slept **2 hours less per day** than non-carriers.
- **NPSR1 (Y206H):** Carriers sleep ~3 hours (180 min) less than controls.
- **mGluR1 loss-of-function:** ~100 min less sleep vs wild-type.

### No observed detriment

Twin study: carrier twin 299.3 min vs. non-carrier twin 364.7 min, yet "minimal behavioral and metabolic impairment in response to sleep loss" and nearly identical BMI. Mouse models show increased resistance to neurodegeneration.

### Mechanism

Most mutations (DEC2, ADRB1, NPSR1) enhance **wake-promoting signals** rather than reducing actual sleep need — "the nature of natural short sleep is actually increased wake period rather than reduced sleep."

## Notable quotes
