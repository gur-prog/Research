## Content gaps still open

- **Chronotype effects on optimal sleep window.** The NSS genetics cover one axis of individual variation, but morning-lark vs. night-owl chronotype is a separate and more common source of variation that this corpus doesn't address. *Needs:* chronotype review specifically on sleep window placement (not timing research for shift workers).
- **Sleep need changes with age.** The 7–8h optimum is for general adults; younger adults may need more and older adults slightly less. *Needs:* age-stratified sleep-need review.
- **Wearable validation in real-world home settings.** Fact 9–11 come from a single-night lab study. Longitudinal home accuracy is unknown. *Needs:* multi-night home validation study.
- **CBT-I specific protocols for healthy student populations.** The meta-analysis identifies the active ingredients but doesn't give a specific protocol a student could follow. *Needs:* published self-administered CBT-I manual or guided self-help protocol comparison.
- **Caffeine timing — specific cutoffs.** Parent topic covered energy drinks; this subtopic didn't dig into the half-life / cutoff-time question ("don't drink caffeine after 2 PM" advice). *Needs:* caffeine pharmacokinetics + sleep study.
- **Alcohol as a sleep aid vs. sleep disruptor.** Common student pitfall, uncovered here. *Needs:* alcohol and sleep architecture review.
- **Exercise timing — does evening exercise hurt sleep?** Fact 17 lists exercise as an active ingredient but doesn't say when. *Needs:* exercise-timing and sleep review.
- **Light exposure protocols.** Light is the strongest circadian cue but not explicitly covered by this corpus. *Needs:* circadian light-therapy/morning-light review.
- **Melatonin and magnesium supplementation.** Common student interventions, not covered. *Needs:* supplementation meta-analyses.
