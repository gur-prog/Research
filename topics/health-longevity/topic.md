# Health and Longevity

## Goal

Understand the evidence-based fundamentals of healthspan and longevity well enough to build a daily routine — what to eat, how to move, what to avoid — that a normal person can actually stick to on a normal budget.

## Follow-up goal

Use this research to redesign my day-to-day habits and routines — workout, eating, sleep, work hours, study hours — into a single integrated routine that supports health and longevity **without sabotaging the cognitively demanding path to becoming an electrical engineer**. The products (meal plan, workout routine, rulebook) should be shaped for someone who spends long hours at a desk studying and working on EE problems, needs sustained focus, and can't afford routines that wreck sleep, cost too much time, or trade long-term health for short-term productivity (or vice versa).

Concretely: I'll adopt the resulting meal plan + workout routine + rulebook as a 90-day pilot, track how I feel (energy, focus, sleep, mood, strength), and keep what works.

## Research questions

Each of these must be answered well by at least one strong-trust source before the research is "done".

1. What daily/weekly eating pattern supports longevity, is affordable on a student/early-career budget, and also supports sustained cognitive performance for long study/work sessions?
2. What weekly workout routine (aerobic + resistance) delivers the longevity benefits in the **least total time per week**, so it fits around heavy study hours? Specifically: can I get most of the benefit with ~3 sessions/week?
3. How much sleep do I actually need, and what sleep hygiene practices have the strongest evidence for both longevity and next-day cognitive performance?
4. How should I structure a long desk/study day — break cadence, micro-movement, posture, eye strain, lighting — to avoid the health cost of prolonged sedentary cognitive work?
5. What stress-management and recovery practices (meditation, nature exposure, social connection, deload time) have real evidence behind them for both longevity AND sustained focus?
6. Given limited time and willpower, which of these habit changes yields the biggest return on effort? What's the Pareto-optimal order to adopt them in?
7. What should I explicitly **avoid** that's common among engineering students (energy drinks, all-nighters, junk food, chronic sitting, social isolation) and what's the evidence on how much damage each one actually does?

## Scope — in

- Nutrition patterns associated with long healthspan (not fad diets)
- Physical activity: aerobic and resistance training for general population
- Sleep fundamentals
- Modifiable lifestyle factors (smoking, alcohol, stress)
- What's affordable and practical for a non-athlete

## Scope — out

- Experimental longevity interventions (rapamycin, NAD boosters, etc.)
- Supplements beyond basic evidence-backed ones
- Genetic testing / personalized medicine
- Extreme protocols only viable for enthusiasts

## Intended products

- `meal-plan` — a week of affordable meals consistent with the research
- `rulebook` — short do's and don'ts distilled from the evidence

## Constraints

- **Source target:** 3 (low for smoke test — raise for real runs)
- **Credibility floor:** prefer `peer-reviewed | gov-edu | reputable-media`; allow `blog` only from recognized experts
- **Recency:** prefer sources from the last 10 years
- **Language:** English
- **Budget:** meal plan should assume a modest grocery budget
